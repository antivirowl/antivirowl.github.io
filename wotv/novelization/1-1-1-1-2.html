Book 1: War of Lion's Valley
Part 1: Waking of the Lion
The air was still.
Heavy, with ill intent.
And Schuzelt did not like this road.
         High above, half-hidden among dripping leaves, a bandit scout crouched motionless. Rain slid from his hood as he watched the procession pass beneath him. His eyes narrowed when he caught sight of the crest upon the carriage.

     Hourne.
         The scout withdrew without a sound, slipping from the branches and signaling ahead.
    The forest gradually thinned. Trees gave way to open ground where wind and rain struck unobstructed. Ahead, stone rose sharply on either side as the road narrowed into a rocky mountain pass—barren, exposed, offering little cover.
    Rain fell harder now, striking stone and armor alike, drumming against steel and leather as both sides faced one another, motionless but poised to strike.
From within the carriage came Machérie’s calm voice.
“Is something the matter, Schuzelt?”

He did not turn.

Chapter 1.1
Twin Princes
Rain slid along the edge of his blade as he answered.
“Worry not. I shall handle these ruffians in short order.”

A pause followed.
For a moment, even the rain seemed to soften.
“I do so miss our castle,” Machérie said quietly.

“Princess…”
His tone carried warning more than reproach.

“I know,” she replied.
“It’s for the good of the kingdom.”

“My sympathies, my lady.”

She hesitated before speaking again,
her voice touched with longing.
“If only I’d been born a man, then I could rush headlong into battle with you.”
